Conjoint analysis is based on showing multiple combinations, at random, each time varying some parameters.

For example, in our study we have shown different options to receive a class action lawsuit compensation on the following parameters:

  • Is the compensation given by push or pull (do you have to actively ask for it in order to get it).
  • Is the compensation given by credit cart/bank cheque/coupon/refund at next purchase/complementary product.
  • What is the compensation amount (at four levels: 25 ILS, 50 ILS, 75 ILS, 100 ILS)

Each respondent received five randomized sets (each set with three packages to choose the best one).

For example, he might have been requested to choose the best option out of the following three:

  1. You will be given a compensation of 25 ILS, to your credit card, and you don't have to ask for it; or
  2. You will be given a compensation of 50 ILS, as a coupon, but you have to ask for it in order to get it; or
  3. You will be given a compensation of 100 ILS, as a complementary product that the company will choose for you, and you have to ask for it in order to get it.

The data was then analyzed using logistic regression to determine the relative influence of each parameter on the attractiveness of the package.
